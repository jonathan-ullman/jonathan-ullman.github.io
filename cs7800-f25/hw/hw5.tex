\problemitem
In the \textsc{HittingSet} problem, we are given a number $k$ and a collection of subsets $A_1,\dots,A_m \subseteq \{1,\dots,n\}$ and our goal is to determine if there exists a hitting \emph{hitting set} $H \subseteq \{1,\dots,n\}$ of size at most $k$ such that $H$ contains at least one element from every subset $A_i$.  That is, we want $|H| \leq k$ and $H\cap A_i \neq \emptyset$ for every $i = 1,\dots,m$.  

Prove that \textsc{HittingSet} is NP-complete.

\problemitem
In the \textsc{ZeroWeightCycle} problem, we are given a directed graph, weighted graph $G = (V,E, \{w_e\})$ and the goal is to decide if $G$ contains a cycle of weight $0$.  That is, a cycle of edges $C$ such that $\sum_{e \in C} w_e = 0$.

Prove that \textsc{ZeroWeightCycle} is NP-complete.\footnote{\textbf{Hint:} Try reducing \textsc{SubsetSum} to \textsc{ZeroWeightCycle}.}

\end{enumerate}
